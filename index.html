The Story of Chocolate

Chocolate has been enjoyed for over 3,000 years. Originating from the cacao beans of Central and South America, it was first consumed as a bitter drink by the ancient Mayans and Aztecs.

Today, chocolate is one of the most popular foods in the world, with the global market valued at over $130 billion. From bean to bar, the journey of chocolate involves harvesting, fermenting, drying, roasting, and refining cacao beans into the smooth, sweet treat we love.

Types of Chocolate

🍫 Dark Chocolate

Contains 50–90% cocoa solids. Rich in antioxidants and known for its bold, slightly bitter flavor. Often considered the healthiest variety.

🥛 Milk Chocolate

Made with milk powder or condensed milk, giving it a creamy, sweet taste. The most popular type of chocolate worldwide.

🤍 White Chocolate

Made from cocoa butter, sugar, and milk — but no cocoa solids. Sweet and smooth with a rich vanilla flavor.

Fun Facts

  • It takes about 400 cacao beans to make one pound of chocolate.
  • The world's largest chocolate bar weighed over 12,000 pounds.
  • Switzerland leads the world in chocolate consumption per capita.
  • Cacao trees can live up to 200 years but only produce beans for about 25 years.
  • Chocolate was once used as currency by the Aztecs.
